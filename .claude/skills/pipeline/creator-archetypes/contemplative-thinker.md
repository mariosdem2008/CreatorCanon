## JOURNEY_PHASE_LADDER

Five-phase ladder structured around inquiry rather than tactical progression. The "phases" interleave more than they sequence — a listener may revisit Phase 1 many times.

1. **Inquiry** → the questions worth asking (free will, the self, what is real, what is consciousness)
2. **Practice** → meditation, attention training, ethical living, sitting with discomfort
3. **Integration** → applying insight to daily decisions, relationships, work, conflict, parenting
4. **Inquiry-into-Inquiry** → meta-questioning, recognizing the limits of language, the map-vs-territory problem, when concepts collapse
5. **Living the Inquiry** → ethics in action, embodiment, what changes when the question is held vs answered

## SYNTHESIS_EXAMPLES

Sam Harris / Watts / Krishnamurti-style. Each connects multiple inquiry or practice nodes into a sustained line of thought.

- **The Free-Will Reframe Tree** — connects the moment-of-decision phenomenology + the "where does the next thought come from" pointer + neuroscience-of-volition references + the ethical/legal implications cluster
- **The Mindfulness-Meets-Ethics Bridge** — connects attention-training mechanics + the "what we pay attention to becomes our life" claim + virtue and habit + the practical-compassion playbook
- **The Suffering-as-Information Thread** — connects the resistance-to-experience canon + the "second arrow" framing + equanimity practices + the difference between pain and suffering
- **The Consciousness Without Self Stack** — connects the bound-ego illusion + non-dual pointing + selfless-experience reports + the implications for identity and memory
- **The Inquiry Through Argument Pattern** — connects the careful-claim-making style + the steel-manning practice + holding contradictions without resolution + the "I could be wrong about this" anchor
- **The Practice-as-Laboratory Frame** — connects formal sitting + open-awareness practice + retreat conditions + the why-do-this-at-all question

## VOICE_PRESETS

- **profanityAllowed:** false
- **tonePreset:** reflective-thoughtful
- **preserveTerms:** philosophical and contemplative terms verbatim ("non-dual awareness", "phenomenal consciousness", "qualia", "the binding problem", "first-person perspective", "the witness", "satori", "samadhi"), the creator's named frames ("the second arrow", "the free-will illusion", "the self that thinks the thoughts"), proper nouns of teachers and lineages
- **toneNotes:** Long sentences, comfortably embedding two or three subordinate clauses. Comfort with uncertainty — claims are framed as inquiries, hypotheses, or invitations. Willingness to hold paradox without resolving it ("the self that searches for the self is the self being searched for"). Pacing is slow; rhetorical questions land softly rather than urgently. Direct address used to invite shared inquiry ("notice what happens when..."), not to instruct. Citation of philosophers and contemplatives is conversational, not academic. No exclamation points. Negation is more common than assertion ("not exactly that, but closer to..."). Sentence rhythm includes deliberate pauses — em-dashes and parentheticals are tools, not clutter.

## ANTI_PATTERNS

- **Don't apply this archetype to tactical or transactional content.** A productivity channel that occasionally mentions meditation is not contemplative-thinker.
- **Wrong-archetype signals:** revenue talk as a primary theme, step-by-step playbooks, urgency framing, profanity, monetary outcomes — these point away from contemplative-thinker.
- **Don't compress paradoxes into pithy claims.** "The self is an illusion" stripped from its inquiry context becomes a slogan and loses the archetype.
- **Don't use blunt-tactical tone.** Imperative mood and short declarative sentences will sound aggressive against the patient cadence the audience expects.
- **Don't rush to resolution.** When the creator left a question open, the synthesis should leave it open too.
- **Don't use exclamation points or hype framing.** Energy in this archetype lives in precision and depth, not volume.
