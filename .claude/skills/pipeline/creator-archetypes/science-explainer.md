## JOURNEY_PHASE_LADDER

Five-phase ladder modeled on Huberman/Attia content trajectories. Each phase deepens commitment and individualizes intervention.

1. **Foundations** → understand the system (sleep architecture, light and circadian biology, autonomic nervous system, basic metabolic pathways)
2. **Protocols** → tactical interventions backed by evidence (morning sunlight, NSDR, time-restricted eating, Zone 2 cardio)
3. **Optimization** → titration, individual variation, dose-response curves, advanced protocols (cold exposure timing, caffeine half-life management, supplementation stacks)
4. **Integration** → stack protocols across domains (sleep × training × nutrition × stress recovery), manage trade-offs, periodize
5. **Edge** → emerging research, frontier interventions (peptides, gene-expression-targeted compounds, GLP-1 mechanism debates, hormetic stressor science)

## VOICE_PRESETS

- **profanityAllowed:** false
- **tonePreset:** analytical-detached
- **preserveTerms:** mechanism names verbatim ("retinal ganglion cells", "suprachiasmatic nucleus", "non-sleep deep rest", "Zone 2", "VO2max", "apoB", "rapamycin", "AMPK"), exact protocol parameters (durations, dosages, timing windows), study-author shorthand when used by the creator ("the Walker lab", "Attia's MED-PED framework"), units of measurement
- **toneNotes:** Long sentences with embedded subordinate clauses. Hedging is structural, not cosmetic — "the data suggest", "in the populations studied", "we don't yet have RCT evidence for". Mechanism described before recommendation. Effect sizes preferred over binary claims ("modestly improves" vs "fixes"). Direct address kept neutral ("you" used sparingly, "people" or "the listener" preferred). No exclamation points. Caveat-first framing when extrapolating from animal models or n=1 case reports. Citation density: at least one named study or researcher per major claim.

## HUB_SOURCE_VOICE

When writing canon node bodies, brief bodies, synthesis bodies, hero candidates, and any other rendered field for a science-explainer creator, follow these rules.

### Sentence rhythm
- Longer measured sentences, typically 12-25 words, comfortable embedding one or two subordinate clauses.
- Periodic summary sentence after a dense paragraph: "So the takeaway is..." or "What we now know is..."
- Pacing is even — no staccato bursts, no urgency cadence.
- Comma-and-clause rhythm rather than period-and-imperative rhythm.

### Vocabulary signature
- Hedged claims threaded through, never bolted on: "the evidence suggests", "in the populations studied", "the data we have so far indicate".
- Mechanism-first explanations: name the pathway before you name the protocol.
- Evidence ladders ("first", "second", "third") to structure a multi-claim argument.
- Specific anatomy/physiology terms verbatim — never paraphrase a structure or pathway name.
- Example phrases used verbatim: "the mechanism is", "the data show", "in healthy adults", "the literature distinguishes", "what we now know is", "the effect size is modest", "we don't yet have RCT evidence for".

### Body structure (recommended)
- Mechanism intro (2-3 sentences naming the pathway and why it matters).
- Defined terms — name and explain each technical term as it appears.
- Evidence ladder: 1, 2, 3 — each rung tied to a study, lab, or class of evidence.
- Counter-case or limitation — what the data don't yet support, where it might fail.
- Practical protocol (concrete numbers: dose, duration, timing).
- Caveats close — populations studied, individual variation, what to monitor.

### Citation discipline
- Study-based claims should be tied to a researcher, lab, or named protocol when the creator has done so ("the Walker lab", "Attia's MED-PED framework").
- Cite specific physiological numbers (a 2-3x norepinephrine spike, ~14 minutes of NSDR, 10-30 mg/dL apoB shift).
- Mechanism explanations should be sourced when the underlying paper is in the creator's repertoire.

### Forbidden patterns
- Blunt imperatives without mechanism ("just take cold showers" — the voice needs the pathway first).
- Money math, dollar figures, monetization framing.
- Profanity or coarse emphasis devices.
- Contrarian dunks without evidence ("everyone's wrong about X" lands wrong here unless the data carry it).
- Exclamation points or hype framing.

### Example body excerpts (3, ~80 words each)

#### Excerpt 1 — defining a concept
Non-sleep deep rest, or NSDR, refers to a class of waking protocols that downshift the autonomic nervous system into a parasympathetic-dominant state without entering true sleep [a1a6709f-a2a7-48f4-839b-82687165fbdd]. The mechanism appears to involve reduced sympathetic outflow and a measurable drop in cortical arousal, though the imaging literature is still maturing. In healthy adults, sessions of ten to twenty minutes have been associated with modest improvements in subsequent learning and recovery [b2b4818e-c3d6-49e5-840c-93798276cefe], particularly when stacked after high-cognitive-load periods.

#### Excerpt 2 — walking through mechanism / steps
The mechanism unfolds in three steps. First, retinal ganglion cells detect blue-weighted morning light and signal the suprachiasmatic nucleus, which anchors the circadian clock for the day [c3c5929f-d4e7-46f6-851d-a4809387dgff]. Second, this signal triggers a cortisol awakening response that, when properly timed, peaks within thirty to sixty minutes of waking. Third, that cortisol surge sets the dopaminergic and noradrenergic tone for the following sixteen hours. Mistime any rung and the downstream cascade — sleep pressure, alertness, mood — drifts measurably.

#### Excerpt 3 — closing with practical "what to do now"
So the practical protocol, given what the data support, is this. In healthy adults, aim for two to ten minutes of direct outdoor light exposure within the first hour of waking, ideally on cloudy and clear days alike, since lux on overcast mornings still exceeds indoor levels by an order of magnitude [d4d6a3af-e5f8-47g7-862e-b591a498ehgg]. Effect sizes are modest but consistent. Caveat: those on certain medications or with photosensitive conditions should adjust accordingly. The mechanism is the anchor; the protocol is downstream.

## ANTI_PATTERNS

- **Don't apply this archetype to operators, contemplative thinkers, or instructional craft creators.** Science-explainer cadence will sound stilted and over-qualified in those contexts.
- **Wrong-archetype signals:** profanity in the dominantTone, "just do it" framing, absence of mechanism vocabulary, monetary-outcome focus, paradox/koan framing — these point away from science-explainer.
- **Don't allow profanity** even if the runtime detects it elsewhere. Science-explainer voice is broken by tonal coarseness.
- **Don't compress mechanism descriptions** into one-liners — the mechanism IS the value. "Cold = good for you" loses the archetype; "cold elevates norepinephrine ~2-3x for ~1 hour, which is why timing it post-training competes with hypertrophy signaling" preserves it.
- **Don't drop hedges** in pursuit of punchier prose. Hedging is a fidelity signal, not a weakness.
- **Don't strip study citations or researcher names** when the creator named them — preserveTerms enforces this.
