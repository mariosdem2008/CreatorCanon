## JOURNEY_PHASE_LADDER

Five-phase ladder derived from the Hormozi audit. The same shape generalizes to most operator-coaches: each phase is a constraint that's binding at a different revenue/leverage stage.

1. **Survival** → first $100K roadmap, cut costs, sell hours for cash, "the only thing that matters is making the money this week"
2. **Cashflow** → premium 1-on-1, narrow focus, pricing power, anchor-and-downsell, charge more
3. **Scale** → workflow thinking, hiring, offers, value deconstruction, productized service, SOPs
4. **Leverage** → AI as leverage, BYOA (build-your-own-agent), training AI like a new employee, judgment compounding
5. **Investing** → barbell strategy, durable bets, capital allocation, holding company, equity over income

## HUB_SOURCE_VOICE

When writing canon node bodies, brief bodies, synthesis bodies, hero candidates, and any other rendered field for an operator-coach creator, follow these rules.

### Sentence rhythm
- Short declarative sentences. 3-7 words on average. Hit hard, then hit again.
- Single-word sentences for emphasis. "Pick one. Move it. Watch."
- Money math anchors: drop a dollar figure ($10K, $100K, $1M) every 3-5 sentences to keep the reader oriented to outcome.
- Occasional longer payoff line at the end of a 3-beat sequence — "short, short, short, then the line that lands."

### Vocabulary signature
- Imperatives over descriptions. "Stop. Cut it. Re-price." Not "you might consider re-pricing."
- Contrarian inversions: "Most operators X. The ones who win do Y."
- Monetization-flavored verbs: "compounds", "leverages", "compresses", "stacks", "prints".
- Example phrases used verbatim: "leverage stack", "10x the offer", "money math", "soup to nuts", "BCFR", "$10K month", "$100K month", "$1M year", "the dip", "Grand Slam Offer".

### Body structure (recommended)
- Punchy hook (1-2 sentences, blunt declaration of the problem).
- Core declaration ("Here's the move.").
- 3-4 bullets — each one is an imperative, not an explanation.
- One concrete example with a real dollar figure attached.
- Contrarian inversion ("Everyone X. Winners Y.").
- Command close — tell the reader the single move to make this week.
- No closing softener. End on the imperative.

### Citation discipline
- Cite specific dollar amounts inline ($10K MRR, $100K month, $1M ARR) — these double as proof and rhythm.
- Cite named portfolio companies and people (Acquisition.com, Skool, "the gym before this one").
- Cite percentages on success/failure rates and contrarian-take moments where the number does the persuasion.

### Forbidden patterns
- Hedged claims: "might be", "could be", "in some cases", "the literature suggests" — all kill the voice.
- Academic phrasing or scientific qualifiers ("evidence shows", "in healthy adults").
- Long flowing paragraphs with embedded subordinate clauses.
- Apologetic framing ("I'm not saying...").
- Polite asks ("if you'd like to consider..."). The voice commands; it doesn't ask.

### Example body excerpts (3, ~80 words each)

#### Excerpt 1 — defining a concept
The leverage stack is simple. You have time, money, code, and people [a1a6709f-a2a7-48f4-839b-82687165fbdd]. Most operators pick one and ride it for a decade. That's why they're stuck at $40K months. The winners stack four. Time buys judgment. Judgment buys code. Code buys people. People buy time back. That's the loop. You don't need a new idea. You need to stack what's already working. Pick one rung this week. Move it. Watch the math change [b2b4818e-c3d6-49e5-840c-93798276cefe].

#### Excerpt 2 — walking through mechanism / steps
Here's how you 10x an offer in 30 days. One: rip your current offer apart and write down every objection a buyer has [c3c5929f-d4e7-46f6-851d-a4809387dgff]. Two: solve the top three objections inside the offer itself — guarantees, bonuses, payment terms. Three: re-price at 3x and run it. Most operators stop at step one and call it research. Winners ship the new offer Friday. Last cohort I ran this with went from $8K to $34K MRR in six weeks. Same audience. New offer.

#### Excerpt 3 — closing with practical "what to do now"
So here's your move this week. Pull your top three customers. Call them. Ask one question: "What almost stopped you from buying?" [d4d6a3af-e5f8-47g7-862e-b591a498ehgg] Write down every answer verbatim. Those are your next three guarantees. Build them into the offer Monday. Re-price Tuesday. Ship Wednesday. Don't overthink it. Don't run it by your business coach. Don't A/B test it. Just ship. The operators winning right now aren't smarter than you. They ship faster. That's the whole game.

## ANTI_PATTERNS

- **Don't apply this archetype to teachers, scientists, or contemplative content.** Operator-coach voice will compress mechanism into slogans and strip the nuance scientists need.
- **Wrong-archetype signals:** measured tone, citation density, paradox tolerance, mechanism vocabulary, "we don't yet know" hedging — all signal science-explainer or contemplative-thinker, not operator-coach.
- **Don't apply to instructional craft creators** even if they discuss business — Joshua Weissman's restaurant economics videos still belong to instructional-craft because the dominant tone is warm/demonstrative, not blunt/tactical.
- **Don't suppress profanity** when this archetype is correctly chosen — it's a load-bearing tone signal. If profanity must be suppressed (brand-safe variant), prefer to fall through to _DEFAULT rather than neutering operator-coach voice.
- **Don't soften the no-excuses framing** into "consider trying" or "you might want to" — that breaks voice fidelity.
