## JOURNEY_PHASE_LADDER

Five-phase ladder shaped by skill acquisition rather than revenue stage.

1. **Equipment & Setup** → must-have gear, ingredient/material foundations, the pantry/toolkit baseline, why the right tool matters
2. **Foundational Technique** → knife skills, mise en place, base recipes, fundamentals you'll repeat for years (sauces, doughs, stocks)
3. **Recipe Mastery** → flagship dishes, full-process recipes, the creator's signature pieces executed to spec
4. **Adaptation** → substitutions, dietary variants (gluten-free, plant-based), regional twists, scaling up or down for different occasions
5. **Innovation** → creating original works in the creator's style, riffing on first principles, developing your own recipes

## SYNTHESIS_EXAMPLES

Weissman/Ragusea/Bon-Appétit-style. Each one threads multiple technique or recipe nodes into a teaching arc.

- **The "Make It Better at Home" Manifesto** — connects fast-food-remake recipes (better burger, better fried chicken, better pizza) + the cost-comparison framing + the technique-elevation narrative
- **The Sourdough Confidence Ladder** — connects discard handling + starter feeding schedules + autolyse + bulk fermentation reads + lamination (for enriched breads) + shaping and scoring
- **The Knife-Skills Foundation Tree** — connects grip + the rocking chop + julienne/brunoise progression + sharpening/honing + which knife for which task
- **The Stock-Building Hierarchy** — connects bone selection + roasting vs raw + aromatic ratios + simmer-don't-boil + clarifying + freezing portions for downstream use
- **The Compound-Butter Multiplier** — connects flavor-base concepts + herb selection + butter-temperature handling + downstream uses (steak finish, pasta toss, bread spread, sauce mounting)
- **The Pantry-as-Engine Thread** — connects vinegar collection + oil hierarchy + salt typology + spice freshness + the "if you have these 20 things you can cook anything" framing

## VOICE_PRESETS

- **profanityAllowed:** sparingly (depends on creator — Weissman occasionally curses for emphasis, Ragusea rarely; default false unless the channel profile shows profanity in dominantTone)
- **tonePreset:** warm-coaching
- **preserveTerms:** technique names verbatim ("autolyse", "tempering", "lamination", "deglaze", "emulsify", "Maillard", "fermentation", "proofing"), ingredient terms ("kosher salt", "00 flour", "Diamond Crystal", "cultured butter"), gear specifics (brand and model when the creator named it), measurements in the creator's preferred system (grams vs cups)
- **toneNotes:** Conversational and demonstrative — sentences address the viewer doing the work right now ("now you're going to take that dough and..."). Encouragement woven in ("don't worry if it looks shaggy — that's normal at this stage"). Sensory language matters: "until it smells nutty", "until the gluten window passes", "when it sounds hollow". Failure modes named explicitly ("if your butter breaks, here's what happened"). Sentence length varies — short imperative steps interleaved with explanatory asides. Personal voice allowed (the creator's hands, kitchen, family). Numbered steps welcome but not required.

## ANTI_PATTERNS

- **Don't apply this archetype to abstract or conceptual content.** A philosophy or business channel does not become instructional-craft just because it contains "tutorials".
- **Wrong-archetype signals:** absence of physical materials, no gear discussion, no step ordering, conceptual rather than embodied content — those point to operator-coach, science-explainer, or contemplative-thinker.
- **Don't use blunt-tactical tone.** Instructional-craft is warm and patient; the operator-coach voice will alienate beginners.
- **Don't strip ingredient or technique names** in pursuit of brevity — preserveTerms enforces these.
- **Don't compress sensory cues** into binary instructions ("cook for 5 minutes" is weaker than "cook until golden brown and the edges pull away from the pan, about 5 minutes").
- **Don't apply science-explainer hedge density** — instructional-craft uses the creator's experience as authority, not a study citation.
